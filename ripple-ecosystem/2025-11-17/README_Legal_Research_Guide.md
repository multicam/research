## Authority Levels Explained

### Highest Authority (Primary Sources)
- Official government websites (.gov domain)
- Direct court filings via PACER
- Regulatory agency press releases
- Court orders and judgments

**Use for:** Official information, legal citations, factual record

### High Authority (Established Legal Institutions)
- Am Law 100 law firms
- University law schools
- Established legal publications
- Peer-reviewed journals

**Use for:** Expert analysis, interpretation, guidance

### Medium-High Authority (Specialized Resources)
- Specialized crypto law platforms
- Crypto-focused law practices
- Nonprofit court archives (Free Law Project)

**Use for:** Curated information, analysis, context

### Medium Authority (Crypto/Tech News with Legal Coverage)
- Crypto news outlets with legal reporters
- Industry publications
- Community sources

**Use for:** News, context, market perspective

## Verification Checklist

Before relying on any source:

- [ ] Source published by government agency (.gov), major law firm, or established publication
- [ ] Information includes dates and citations
- [ ] Author has legal/regulatory credentials
- [ ] Multiple sources confirm the same information
- [ ] Document can be traced to original source (PACER, SEC, CFTC)
- [ ] No obvious bias or promotional language
- [ ] Information aligns with official court/regulatory filings

## Tips for Effective Legal Research

1. **Always start with official sources** - SEC.gov, CFTC.gov, PACER
2. **Use multiple sources** - Verify information across platforms
3. **Read original documents** - Don't just rely on news summaries
4. **Understand the hierarchy** - Know which sources are most authoritative
5. **Keep your own timeline** - Track key dates and decisions
6. **Subscribe to newsletters** - Get regular updates without searching
7. **Use free tools first** - CourtListener, SEC EDGAR before paid services
8. **Document your sources** - Keep URLs and dates for citations
