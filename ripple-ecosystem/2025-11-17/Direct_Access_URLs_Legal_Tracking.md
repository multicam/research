### Direct PACER Access Methods

**Method 1: PACER Case Locator (Recommended)**
```
https://pcl.uscourts.gov/
```
- National index for federal cases
- Search by case number: 1:20-cv-10832
- Search by party: "Ripple Labs Inc."
- Real-time docket updates
- Individual document cost: $0.10 per page

**Method 2: Direct Southern District of New York PACER**
```
https://pacer.uscourts.gov/find-case/search-specific-court
```
- Select: U.S. District Court - Southern District of New York
- Enter case number: 20-cv-10832 or 1:20-cv-10832
- Or search by party name: "Ripple"

**Method 3: CourtListener RECAP Archive (Free)**
```
https://www.courtlistener.com/recap/
```
- Search for case: SEC v. Ripple
- Free access to millions of PACER documents
- Docket tracking available with browser extension (15 free tracked dockets)
- Install RECAP browser extension for automatic document uploads to free archive

**Method 4: CourtListener Direct Case Link**
```
https://www.courtlistener.com/docket/?q=SEC+v+Ripple
```
- Search results for Ripple-related cases
- Multiple case matches will appear
- Click on specific case for full docket

### Accessing Specific Key Documents

- Access through Case Locator: 1:20-cv-10832

## TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES

### PACER Access Issues
- **Problem:** Cannot find case
- **Solution:** Verify court is Southern District of New York; case number is 1:20-cv-10832
- **Support:** (202) 502-8440

### CourtListener Not Loading Documents
- **Problem:** Some PACER documents not available
- **Solution:** Document may not yet be in RECAP archive; purchase directly from PACER
- **Cost:** $0.10 per page

### SEC Press Release Not Found
- **Problem:** Cannot locate specific announcement
- **Solution:** Visit https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/ and search by date range and keyword
- **Alternative:** Use SEC press release archive at https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/press-releases/

### Missing Recent Filings
- **Problem:** Latest court documents not visible
- **Solution:** PACER and CourtListener update in real-time; refresh page
- **Timeline:** Federal courts typically file documents within 24 hours
