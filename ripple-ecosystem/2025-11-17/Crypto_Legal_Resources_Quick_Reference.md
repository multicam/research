## DOCUMENT ACCESS METHODS

### For Ripple v. SEC Court Filings

**Method 1: PACER (Official, Comprehensive)**
- URL: https://pacer.uscourts.gov/
- Court: U.S. District Court, Southern District of New York
- Cost: $0.10 per page
- Contains: All briefs, motions, orders, evidence

**Method 2: CourtListener RECAP (Free)**
- URL: https://www.courtlistener.com/recap/
- Cost: Free (with browser extension for docket tracking)
- Contains: Millions of PACER documents
- Coverage: Less comprehensive but growing

**Method 3: Law Library Guides**
- Harvard: https://guides.library.harvard.edu/
- Stanford: https://guides.law.stanford.edu/dockets/freeresources
- University of Michigan: https://libguides.law.umich.edu/

### For SEC Filings & Press Releases

**SEC Newsroom Press Releases**
- https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/
- Filter for enforcement actions and settlements

**SEC EDGAR Database**
- https://www.sec.gov/edgar/search/
- Search: "Ripple Labs Inc." or case-related companies

## BEST PRACTICES FOR LEGAL RESEARCH

1. **Start with Official Sources** - SEC.gov, CFTC.gov, PACER (primary authority)
2. **Cross-Reference Multiple Sources** - Verify information across platforms
3. **Read Original Documents** - Access briefs and filings directly through PACER
4. **Follow Law Firm Analysis** - Sheppard Mullin, Goodwin, Latham & Watkins provide expert interpretation
5. **Subscribe to Alerts** - Email newsletters for consistent updates
6. **Use Free Tools First** - CourtListener, PACER Case Locator before paid services
7. **Track Timeline** - Maintain personal timeline of key developments
8. **Document Sources** - Record source URLs and dates for citations
