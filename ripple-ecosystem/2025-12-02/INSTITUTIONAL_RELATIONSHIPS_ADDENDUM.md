This addendum provides comprehensive analysis of five major institutional players' relationships with Ripple and XRP, revealing a spectrum from active partnership (Mastercard) to complete divergence (Goldman Sachs). ## 1. Mastercard-Ripple Partnership

### Relationship Status: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **ACTIVE PRODUCTION PARTNERSHIP**

### The November 2025 RLUSD Settlement Breakthrough

**Announcement Details:**
- **Date:** November 5, 2025, at Ripple Swell 2025 in New York
- **Partners:** Ripple, Mastercard, WebBank (card issuer), Gemini (crypto exchange)
- **Technology:** RLUSD stablecoin on XRP Ledger (XRPL) for settling Mastercard credit card transactions
- **Significance:** One of the first instances where a regulated U.S. bank (WebBank) will leverage a regulated stablecoin (RLUSD) on a public blockchain (XRPL) for traditional card settlements

**Value Proposition:**
Traditional credit card settlement between merchant banks and card issuers takes 1-3 days. RLUSD on XRPL can move funds nearly instantly, especially across borders. This pilot tests whether blockchain-based stablecoin settlement can replace legacy clearing processes.

**Executive Statement - Sherri Haymond** (Mastercard Global Head of Digital Commercialization):
> "Through our partnerships with Ripple, Gemini, and WebBank, we're using our global payments network to bring regulated, open-loop stablecoin payments into the financial mainstream."

### Critical Technical Distinction: RLUSD vs XRP

**What Mastercard IS Using:**
- **XRP Ledger (XRPL):** The blockchain infrastructure
- **RLUSD:** Ripple's USD-backed stablecoin (1:1 peg, launched December 2024)
- **Current Status:** $1.02 billion market cap, regulated under New York Trust Charter

**What Mastercard is NOT Using:**
- **XRP cryptocurrency token** directly for settlement
- XRP serves as the native asset of XRPL but RLUSD is the actual settlement medium

**Why This Matters:**
Many media reports conflate "Mastercard-Ripple partnership" with "Mastercard using XRP," which is technically incorrect. While RLUSD transactions on XRPL indirectly benefit XRP (through network effects and potential future bridge currency use), Mastercard's current pilot uses RLUSD as the settlement asset.

## 2. R3 Consortium Relationship

### Relationship Status: ⭐⭐ **HISTORICAL PARTNERSHIP → LEGAL BATTLE → TECHNICAL INTEGRATION → STRATEGIC DIVERGENCE**

### The $240-470 Million Settlement

**Original Agreement (September 2016):**
- R3 entered agreement to promote Ripple to its banking consortium
- **Key Term:** R3 received option to purchase up to 5 billion XRP tokens at **$0.0085 per token** before September 2019
- Potential value if exercised at $1 XRP: $4.99 billion in profit

**Relationship Deteriorates (June 2017):**
- Ripple CEO Brad Garlinghouse attempted to terminate options via email to R3 CEO David Rutter
- Reason: Ripple claimed R3 breached commitments within original agreement

**Legal Battle (September 2017 - September 2018):**
- R3 sued Ripple in Delaware and New York for specific performance
- Ripple countersued in California for breach of commitments
- Delaware judge dismissed case against Ripple; litigation continued in California and New York

**Settlement (September 10, 2018):**
- Both parties announced confidential settlement of all litigation
- **Outcome:** R3 purchased 1.04 billion XRP tokens in 2019
- Based on 2019 XRP prices ($0.23-$0.45), R3 paid **between $240 million and $470 million**
- Significant premium over original $0.0085 option price ($8.8 million for 1.04 billion XRP)

**What Was At Stake:**
At its peak in early 2018, XRP reached $3.84. If R3 had exercised the full 5 billion token option at $0.0085, they would have paid $42.5 million for tokens worth $19.2 billion—a $19.16 billion profit. The legal dispute centered on whether R3 maintained those rights.

### Technical Integration: Corda Settler

**December 2018 Launch (Post-Settlement):**
Despite legal battles just months earlier, R3 chose **XRP as the FIRST cryptocurrency supported** for settlement in its Corda Settler universal payments application.

**CEO David Rutter Statement (2019):**
> "This collaboration is a key way to enhance efficiencies in banks and legacy financial establishments. The combination of Corda's distributed registry technology and Ripple money transfer is likely to create many new business cases that were previously impossible."

**Technical Architecture:**
- 4 Corda nodes in setup
- Oracle system hosting: FX rate oracle, XRP settlement oracle
- **Process Flow:**
  1. Obligations in USD converted to XRP using live rates
  2. Party B executes payments
  3. Oracle verifies by checking XRP transaction hash

**Strategic Significance:**
R3's decision to integrate XRP immediately after settling a bitter legal dispute suggests technical merits outweighed personal/institutional animosity. The integration demonstrated that blockchain technology transcends corporate politics.

**2023: XRP Support Deprecated**

- **Current Status:** XRP support effectively discontinued

**Why the Shift?**
- **Technical:** XDC offers trade finance-specific features vs XRP's payment focus
- **Strategic:** XDC partnership provided differentiation from Ripple
- **Commercial:** XDC Network likely offered better partnership terms
- **Competitive:** R3 and Ripple pursuing separate market strategies

**3. Banking Consortium Value**
R3's ability to gather 40+ major banks in 2015-2016 provided Ripple access to decision-makers across the industry. Even though R3 relationship soured, those banking connections laid groundwork for Ripple's current 300+ partnerships.

**4. Technology Agnosticism**
Major banks' willingness to test both R3 Corda and Ripple XRP demonstrates they're technology-agnostic, choosing solutions based on use case fit rather than vendor loyalty.

**5. Parallel Paths to Success**
R3 (CBDCs, tokenized assets) and Ripple (cross-border payments, liquidity) achieving success in different markets proves blockchain ecosystem is large enough for multiple enterprise players.

- **Tier 1 (Public):** USDT, USDC dominate retail and cross-exchange
- **Tier 2 (Institutional):** RLUSD grows for B2B settlements via Mastercard and RippleNet
- **Tier 3 (Bank Internal):** JPM Coin, Citi Token for intra-bank client transactions

**Critical Success Factor:**
RLUSD's success depends on Mastercard pilot proving blockchain settlement delivers measurable cost/time savings. If successful, other card networks and payment processors will integrate RLUSD, creating network effects. If unsuccessful, RLUSD remains niche stablecoin.

### ISO 20022: Infrastructure Upgrade Cycle

**The Opportunity:**
November 2025 SWIFT migration created moment when banks MUST upgrade payment infrastructure. This represents natural inflection point to evaluate blockchain alternatives.

**Traditional Banking Response:**
- Upgrade SWIFT GPI infrastructure
- Implement ISO 20022 messaging
- Maintain correspondent banking relationships
- **Result:** Improved but still slow/expensive cross-border

**Blockchain Integration Response:**
- Implement ISO 20022 messaging
- Add blockchain settlement layer (RippleNet, Stellar, proprietary)
- Maintain SWIFT for corridors lacking blockchain coverage
- **Result:** Fast/cheap where blockchain available, SWIFT fallback

**Hybrid Approach (Most Likely):**
Banks will implement ISO 20022 messaging that works with BOTH SWIFT and blockchain backends. This maintains flexibility to route payments through cheapest/fastest available rail.

**Strategic Implications:**
- Ripple's ISO 20022 compatibility positions as drop-in enhancement to SWIFT vs replacement
- Banks can test blockchain corridors while maintaining SWIFT backup
- Gradual migration reduces risk vs big-bang transformation
